# Governance: OpenLaunch

## Overview

OpenLaunch operates as a community-driven, transparent, and inclusive organization through our annual Coding Party initiatives. Our governance model is designed to ensure that all voices are heard while maintaining the ability to make decisions efficiently and move projects forward effectively.

## Governance Principles

### 1. Transparency
- All governance processes are documented and public
- Decision-making happens in open forums
- Meeting notes and voting records are publicly available
- Financial information is shared openly with the community

### 2. Inclusivity
- All community members have a voice in governance
- Multiple pathways exist for participation
- Decisions consider diverse perspectives and needs
- Barriers to participation are actively identified and removed

### 3. Meritocracy with Equity
- Contributions are recognized regardless of background
- Leadership opportunities are available to all qualified members
- Systemic barriers are acknowledged and addressed
- Success is measured by community impact, not just technical contributions

### 4. Consensus-Driven
- Decisions are made through discussion and consensus when possible
- Voting is used when consensus cannot be reached
- Minority opinions are documented and considered
- Appeals processes exist for contested decisions

## Organizational Structure

### Community Members
All individuals who participate in OpenLaunch in any capacity.

**Rights:**
- Participate in public discussions
- Submit issues and feature requests
- Contribute code, documentation, design, or other work
- Attend community events and meetings
- Vote in community-wide decisions

**Responsibilities:**
- Follow the Code of Conduct
- Respect community guidelines and processes
- Contribute constructively to discussions
- Support newcomers and maintain welcoming environment

### Contributors
Community members who have made meaningful contributions to projects.

**Qualification Criteria:**
- Made at least 3 substantial contributions (code, docs, design, etc.)
- Demonstrated understanding of project goals and values
- Shown commitment to community standards
- Received positive feedback from other contributors

**Additional Rights:**
- Nominate candidates for maintainer roles
- Participate in contributor-only discussions
- Access to contributor resources and tools
- Priority consideration for mentorship opportunities

**Additional Responsibilities:**
- Mentor newcomers and help with onboarding
- Participate in code reviews and quality assurance
- Represent the community positively in external interactions
- Contribute to project planning and decision-making

### Maintainers
Contributors who take ongoing responsibility for specific projects or areas.

**Qualification Criteria:**
- Active contributor for at least 6 months
- Deep understanding of relevant project areas
- Demonstrated leadership and mentorship skills
- Commitment to community values and governance
- Nominated by contributors and approved by existing maintainers

**Rights:**
- Merge pull requests in their areas of responsibility
- Make technical decisions for their projects
- Participate in maintainer meetings and planning
- Access to maintainer tools and resources
- Vote in maintainer-level decisions

**Responsibilities:**
- Ensure project quality and consistency
- Review and merge contributions
- Mentor contributors and help with technical decisions
- Participate in project planning and roadmap development
- Represent projects in cross-project coordination
- Handle conflicts and disputes in their areas

### Core Team
Long-term maintainers who help guide the overall direction of OpenLaunch.

**Qualification Criteria:**
- Active maintainer for at least 1 year
- Significant contributions across multiple projects
- Demonstrated strategic thinking and community leadership
- Strong commitment to community values and long-term vision
- Elected by maintainers and confirmed by community vote

**Rights:**
- Participate in strategic planning and vision setting
- Make organization-wide policy decisions
- Represent Coding Party 2026 in external partnerships
- Access to all organizational resources and information
- Final decision authority in case of unresolved disputes

**Responsibilities:**
- Set strategic direction and long-term vision
- Ensure organizational health and sustainability
- Coordinate between projects and maintainers
- Handle sensitive issues and conflicts
- Represent the organization publicly
- Ensure governance processes are followed and improved

## Decision-Making Processes

### Types of Decisions

#### Project-Level Decisions
**Scope:** Technical decisions within a specific project
**Decision Makers:** Project maintainers
**Process:** Discussion → Consensus → Implementation
**Examples:** Architecture choices, feature prioritization, code standards

#### Cross-Project Decisions
**Scope:** Decisions affecting multiple projects
**Decision Makers:** All maintainers
**Process:** Discussion → Consensus → Community feedback → Implementation
**Examples:** Shared infrastructure, common standards, resource allocation

#### Community-Wide Decisions
**Scope:** Decisions affecting the entire community
**Decision Makers:** All community members
**Process:** Proposal → Discussion → Voting → Implementation
**Examples:** Code of Conduct changes, governance updates, major policy changes

#### Strategic Decisions
**Scope:** Long-term direction and vision
**Decision Makers:** Core team with community input
**Process:** Research → Proposal → Community feedback → Core team decision
**Examples:** Mission changes, major partnerships, organizational restructuring

### Decision-Making Process

#### 1. Proposal Phase
- Anyone can propose a decision through GitHub Discussions
- Proposals must include problem statement, proposed solution, and rationale
- Proposals are tagged with appropriate decision type
- Community members can ask questions and suggest modifications

#### 2. Discussion Phase
- Open discussion period (minimum 1 week for major decisions)
- All perspectives are welcomed and considered
- Proposal may be modified based on feedback
- Alternative solutions can be proposed

#### 3. Consensus Building
- Attempt to reach consensus through discussion
- Facilitators help guide discussion and identify common ground
- Minority opinions are documented and addressed
- If consensus is reached, move to implementation

#### 4. Voting Phase (if consensus not reached)
- Voting is used when consensus cannot be achieved
- Voting period is clearly announced (minimum 1 week)
- All eligible community members can vote
- Results are transparent and binding

#### 5. Implementation Phase
- Decision is documented with rationale
- Implementation plan is created and communicated
- Progress is tracked and reported to community
- Feedback is collected during implementation

### Voting Procedures

#### Eligibility
- **Community Decisions:** All community members
- **Maintainer Decisions:** All maintainers
- **Core Team Decisions:** Core team members

#### Voting Methods
- **Simple Majority:** Most decisions (>50% of votes cast)
- **Supermajority:** Major changes (>66% of votes cast)
- **Unanimous:** Core team membership changes

#### Voting Process
1. **Announcement:** Voting period announced with clear timeline
2. **Information:** All relevant information provided to voters
3. **Voting:** Secure, anonymous voting system used
4. **Results:** Results published with vote counts and percentages
5. **Appeals:** 48-hour period for appeals or challenges

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Role Transitions

#### Becoming a Contributor
- **Process:** Automatic upon making meaningful contributions
- **Timeline:** Immediate recognition after contribution acceptance
- **Requirements:** Follow community guidelines and Code of Conduct

#### Becoming a Maintainer
- **Process:** Nomination by contributors, approval by existing maintainers
- **Timeline:** 2-week nomination and review period
- **Requirements:** Meet qualification criteria and demonstrate commitment

#### Becoming Core Team
- **Process:** Election by maintainers, community confirmation vote
- **Timeline:** Annual elections or as needed for vacant positions
- **Requirements:** Meet qualification criteria and receive community support

#### Role Transitions and Stepping Down
- **Voluntary:** Members can step down from roles at any time
- **Inactive:** Roles may be revoked after 6 months of inactivity
- **Misconduct:** Roles may be revoked for Code of Conduct violations
- **Process:** Transparent process with opportunity for response

### Conflict Resolution

#### Level 1: Direct Resolution
- Encourage direct communication between parties
- Provide mediation resources and guidelines
- Document agreements and resolutions

#### Level 2: Maintainer Intervention
- Maintainers help mediate disputes in their areas
- Formal mediation process with neutral facilitator
- Binding decisions with appeal rights

#### Level 3: Core Team Resolution
- Core team handles escalated conflicts
- Formal investigation and hearing process
- Final decisions with implementation plan

#### Level 4: Community Appeal
- Community vote on contested core team decisions
- Transparent process with all information shared
- Binding community decision

## Organizational Policies

### Code of Conduct Enforcement

#### Reporting Process
- Multiple reporting channels available
- Anonymous reporting options provided
- Confidentiality protections in place
- Clear timeline for response and resolution

#### Investigation Process
- Trained investigators handle all reports
- Fair and thorough investigation procedures
- Due process protections for all parties
- Transparent outcomes (within privacy constraints)

#### Enforcement Actions
- **Warning:** First offense or minor violations
- **Temporary Suspension:** Repeated or moderate violations
- **Permanent Ban:** Severe or repeated violations
- **Appeals Process:** Right to appeal all enforcement actions

### Financial Governance

#### Transparency
- All financial information published quarterly
- Detailed budgets and spending reports
- Donation sources and amounts disclosed
- Financial decisions made through governance process

#### Budget Process
- Annual budget planning with community input
- Quarterly reviews and adjustments
- Emergency spending procedures
- Financial oversight by core team

#### Funding Sources
- Individual donations and sponsorships
- Corporate partnerships and grants
- Service revenue from consulting or training
- Merchandise and event revenue

### Intellectual Property

#### Licensing
- All code released under MIT License
- Documentation under Creative Commons
- Trademarks held by organization
- Clear contributor license agreements

#### Contributor Rights
- Contributors retain copyright to their work
- License grants allow community use
- Attribution requirements respected
- No transfer of ownership required

## Governance Evolution

### Regular Reviews
- **Annual Governance Review:** Comprehensive review of all processes
- **Quarterly Check-ins:** Regular assessment of governance effectiveness
- **Post-Decision Reviews:** Evaluation of decision outcomes
- **Community Feedback:** Ongoing collection of governance feedback

### Amendment Process
- **Proposal:** Any community member can propose changes
- **Discussion:** Open discussion period for all proposals
- **Review:** Core team reviews feasibility and impact
- **Vote:** Community vote on significant changes
- **Implementation:** Gradual rollout with monitoring

### Continuous Improvement
- **Best Practices:** Regular research on governance best practices
- **External Input:** Consultation with governance experts
- **Experimentation:** Pilot programs for new approaches
- **Documentation:** Continuous improvement of governance documentation

- Provide feedback on governance processes
- Vote in community-wide decisions
- Attend governance meetings and events

#### For Contributors
- Nominate maintainer candidates
- Participate in project governance
- Help with conflict resolution

- **Purpose:** Oversee and improve governance processes
- **Members:** Representatives from all community levels

- **Responsibilities:** Process improvement, training, documentation

- **Purpose:** Handle Code of Conduct enforcement

- **Responsibilities:** Investigation, enforcement, appeals

- **Purpose:** Oversee financial management and transparency
- **Members:** Core team members and community volunteers
- **Meetings:** Quarterly meetings with annual planning
- **Responsibilities:** Budget planning, financial oversight, transparency
