# Code of Conduct: Coding Party 2026

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of Coding Party 2026 pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community that embodies our values of collaborative innovation, learning, and mutual respect.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

**Respect and Empathy**
- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Listening actively and seeking to understand before being understood
- Acknowledging and learning from mistakes

**Constructive Communication**
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community
- Communicating clearly and professionally in all interactions

**Collaborative Spirit**
- Supporting fellow community members in their learning and growth
- Sharing knowledge and resources generously
- Celebrating others' achievements and contributions
- Working together toward common goals

**Inclusive Participation**
- Welcoming newcomers and helping them feel included
- Making space for different perspectives and approaches
- Recognizing and valuing diverse contributions
- Creating opportunities for others to participate and lead

**Professional Conduct**
- Taking responsibility for our actions and their consequences
- Honoring commitments and following through on promises
- Maintaining confidentiality when appropriate
- Representing the community positively in external interactions

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered harassment and are unacceptable within our community:

**Harassment and Discrimination**
- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment, intimidation, or stalking
- Discriminatory jokes, language, or behavior based on protected characteristics

**Disruptive Behavior**
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission
- Deliberately disrupting discussions, events, or community activities
- Spamming, flooding, or other forms of disruptive communication
- Persistent unwelcome contact after being asked to stop

**Harmful Content**
- Sharing or promoting harmful, illegal, or inappropriate content
- Encouraging or inciting violence, self-harm, or illegal activities
- Spreading misinformation or deliberately false information
- Violating intellectual property rights or licenses

**Abuse of Power**
- Using position or influence to intimidate, coerce, or harm others
- Retaliating against those who report violations or raise concerns
- Showing favoritism or bias in decision-making processes
- Misusing community resources or privileges

**Professional Misconduct**
- Plagiarism or taking credit for others' work
- Deliberately introducing bugs, security vulnerabilities, or malicious code
- Violating confidentiality agreements or sharing sensitive information
- Engaging in conflicts of interest without proper disclosure

## Community Guidelines

### Communication Standards

**In All Interactions**
- Use clear, respectful, and professional language
- Stay on topic and contribute meaningfully to discussions
- Respect others' time and attention
- Provide context and background when asking questions

**In Code Reviews**
- Focus on the code, not the person
- Provide specific, actionable feedback
- Explain the reasoning behind suggestions
- Acknowledge good work and improvements

**In Discussions and Forums**
- Search existing discussions before creating new ones
- Use descriptive titles and clear descriptions
- Tag relevant people and topics appropriately
- Follow up on your questions and thank those who help

**In Issues and Bug Reports**
- Provide clear, reproducible steps
- Include relevant system information and screenshots
- Use appropriate labels and templates
- Be patient while waiting for responses

### Collaboration Expectations

**For All Contributors**
- Follow established processes and guidelines
- Communicate changes and decisions transparently
- Respect project timelines and commitments
- Ask for help when needed and offer help to others

**For Maintainers and Leaders**
- Model positive behavior and community values
- Make decisions transparently and inclusively
- Provide timely feedback and responses
- Support and mentor community members

**For Newcomers**
- Read documentation and guidelines before asking questions
- Start with small contributions and gradually take on more
- Be patient with the learning process
- Engage respectfully with the community

## Scope and Application

### Where This Code Applies

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including but not limited to:

**Digital Spaces**
- GitHub repositories, issues, and pull requests
- Community discussions and forums
- Chat channels and messaging platforms
- Video calls and virtual meetings
- Social media accounts and posts related to the project

**Physical Spaces**
- In-person meetups and events
- Conferences and workshops
- Co-working sessions and hackathons
- Any gathering of community members

**External Representation**
- When representing the community at external events
- In interviews or media appearances about the project
- When wearing or displaying community branding
- In professional contexts where community affiliation is mentioned

### Personal Conduct

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include:
- Using an official community email address
- Posting via official social media accounts
- Acting as an appointed representative at events
- Speaking on behalf of the community in any capacity

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it by contacting the community team at:

**Primary Contact**: conduct@coding-party-2026.org

**Alternative Contacts**:
- For issues involving core team members: governance@coding-party-2026.org
- For urgent safety concerns: emergency@coding-party-2026.org

**Anonymous Reporting**: You can also submit anonymous reports through our [anonymous reporting form](https://forms.coding-party-2026.org/conduct-report).

### What to Include in Reports

When reporting a violation, please include:
- Your contact information (if not reporting anonymously)
- Names of any individuals involved
- Description of the incident and behavior
- Date, time, and location of the incident
- Any relevant screenshots, logs, or documentation
- Any witnesses to the incident
- Any previous related incidents

### Reporting Process

**Immediate Response**
- All reports are taken seriously and will be reviewed promptly
- You will receive an acknowledgment within 24 hours
- Urgent safety concerns will be addressed immediately

**Investigation**
- A designated team will investigate the report thoroughly
- All parties involved will have an opportunity to provide their perspective
- The investigation will be conducted confidentially and impartially
- Additional information may be requested from reporters or witnesses

**Resolution**
- The investigation team will determine appropriate consequences
- All parties will be informed of the outcome
- Appeals process is available for contested decisions
- Follow-up support will be provided as needed

### Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

#### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

**Example Actions**:
- Private message explaining the issue
- Request for public apology if appropriate
- Guidance on expected behavior going forward

#### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

**Example Actions**:
- Formal written warning
- Temporary restriction from community interactions
- Required completion of community guidelines training
- Monitoring of future behavior

#### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

**Example Actions**:
- 30-90 day suspension from community participation
- Removal from leadership positions during suspension
- Required completion of conduct training before reinstatement
- Probationary period upon return

#### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

**Example Actions**:
- Permanent removal from all community spaces
- Revocation of all community privileges and recognition
- Public notification of ban (if appropriate for safety)
- Legal action if warranted

### Appeals Process

**Right to Appeal**
- Any person subject to enforcement action has the right to appeal
- Appeals must be submitted within 30 days of the decision
- Appeals should include new information or evidence of procedural errors

**Appeals Review**
- Appeals are reviewed by a different panel than the original investigation
- The appeals panel will review all evidence and documentation
- The decision of the appeals panel is final

**Reinstatement**
- Permanent bans may be appealed after one year
- Reinstatement requires demonstration of changed behavior
- Reinstatement decisions consider community safety and trust

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What if I'm not sure if something violates the Code of Conduct?

When in doubt, err on the side of caution and report it. Our team would rather investigate something that turns out to be fine than miss something that makes someone feel unsafe or unwelcome.

### What if the violation involves a community leader?

Reports involving community leaders are handled by different team members to avoid conflicts of interest. You can also use our alternative contact methods or anonymous reporting.

### Will my report be kept confidential?

We maintain confidentiality to the extent possible while still being able to investigate and resolve the issue. Information is only shared with those who need to know to address the situation.

### What if I disagree with the outcome of an investigation?

You have the right to appeal any decision through our appeals process. Appeals are reviewed by a different panel and consider new evidence or procedural concerns.

### How can I help create a positive community environment?

- Model positive behavior in all your interactions
- Speak up when you see problematic behavior
- Welcome and support newcomers
- Participate in community discussions about improvement
- Provide feedback on community policies and processes
